Welcome to the CryptoBot Back-tester!

Change parameters to test how different Moving Average (MA) strategies perform on the BTC-ETH Trading Pair Market

Parameters:
Indicator Which strategy the Bot will use, either a Momentum or Contrarian
Redistribution Frequency: In general, the bot makes sure 50% of your total value remains in your the base currency (BTC) while the other 50% is stored in the quote currency (ETH). This 50-50 position, where your money is equally divided, is known as the Neutral State. The relative price is always changing so the Bot redistributes your money periodically based on the Redistribution Frequency the user specifies in hours. If during this check the price exceeds the MA by a certain amount, a trade occurs. Instead of redistributing the money evenly, the Bot places a majority of the funds into one of the currencies.
Percent Cutoff: This parameter lets the Bot know by what percentage the pair price must deviate from the MA in order to trigger a trade
Percent Exchanged: Whenever the closing price of BTC-ETH deviates the provided percentage from the MA, the Bot places a majority of the funds into one of the currencies instead of keeping the funds in a Neutral State. The percent exchanged must be between 0-50%. Indicating a 50% move means that your entire funds will be
Starting Capital Amount: The initial capital which is provided to the Bot in BTC because that is the base currency

Baselines:
 Ways to assess how well the Bot performed with given User inputs
Hold: In this strategy no trades or redistributions occur and your funds always equal the Starting Capital Amount
Equal Weight (EW): In this strategy the Bot maintains your funds in the 50-50 Neutral state. Ideally the EW strategy beats the Holding strategy
EW with Active MA: In this strategy the Bot maintains generally maintains funds in the 50-50 Neutral state and unless the relative BTC-ETH prices deviates from the MA in which case the Bot places a majority of the funds into one currency. Ideally the EW with MA strategy builds the EW strategy. Usually, if a EW stategy with monentum MA performs better than just the EW strategy, then a EW strategy with contrarian MA will perform worse and vice versa
Sharpe Ratio: Risk-adjusted return measured for both the EW and EW with MA strategy
